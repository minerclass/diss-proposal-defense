Before publishing the atlas as a formal reference resource:

1. Verify each source against the controlling Chapters 1-3 proposal bibliography.
2. Convert entries to APA 7.
3. Add DOI, publisher, journal, edition, or URL fields only after source verification.
4. Mark which sources are cited in Chapter 1, Chapter 2, Chapter 3, appendices, or defense slides.
5. Distinguish conceptual anchors from empirical evidence.

## Public / Private Boundary

GitHub Pages can support:

- proposal defense preparation,
- public explanation of the study,
- non-identifiable conceptual maps,
- source atlas work,
- committee-question rehearsal,
- links to public scholarship.

GitHub Pages should not store or collect:

- identifiable participant information,
- consent records,
- interview recordings or transcripts,
- survey responses,
- raw data exports,
- private researcher memos,
- sensitive district examples,
- staff or student information,
- unpublished private Google Drive links unless intentionally public.

## IRB Caution

Do not recruit participants, distribute surveys, conduct interviews, pilot instruments with potential participants, or collect data through public web infrastructure until the proposal design and IRB pathway are approved.
