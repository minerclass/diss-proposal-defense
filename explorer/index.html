Chapter 1: The Research Foundation

Statement of the Problem

School districts are rushing to adopt policy, purchase tools, or direct acceptable use for Generative AI, but their approaches are structured around a false framing. By viewing AI primarily as an academic integrity/cheating crisis, they miss the deeper threat: how friction-reducing technologies bypass the cognitive and interpretive struggles necessary to restructure human consciousness.

Purpose of the Study

This mixed-methods case study investigates how K–12 practitioners navigate, resist, or design for "pedagogical friction" in the writing process, capturing the perspectives of educators and retrospective accounts of university students who experienced the pre/post-GAI transition.

Significance & Researcher Positionality

As a K-12 instructional technology administrator, the researcher brings a critical, technoskeptical lens that resists technological determinism. This research shifts the debate from a simple binary of "ban vs. embrace" to one of intentional pedagogical design.

Significance: It establishes a clear warrant for the preservation of cognitive struggle (the "generation effect" and "desirable difficulties") in instruction when automated writing assistants make production effortless.
Chapter 2: Review of the Literature & Theoretical Framework

Ong's Media Ecology Stepper

My theoretical framework extends Walter Ong's developmental account of communication media. Select each stage to inspect the transition:

1 Primary Orality Communal, situational, formulaic memory.
2 Literacy Analytical detachment, critical interiority.
3 Secondary Orality Broadcast media retrieves oral performance.
4 Algorithmic Secondary Human-made content; algorithmic circulation.
5 Tertiary Algorithmicity Opaque machines generate and curate.
Current Focus: Algorithmic Secondary Orality
A transitional stage where human-created symbolic content continues, but algorithms determine what reaches which consciousness. Editorial judgment is optimized for engagement rather than truth or shared civic space.
🧠 Noetic Displacement Cognitive and interpretive labor is externalized to external systems, leading to correct outputs without human schema formation.
🗣️ Rhetorical Saturation Synthetic dialogue floods the environment, generating responsive text without human dialogic responsibility or mutual risk.
⚖️ Existential Abstraction Claims are severed from accountable claimants, allowing information to circulate without anyone backing the claims.
Chapter 3: Case Study Methodology

Pragmatist & Case Study Design

The proposed research is organized as a convergent parallel mixed methods design embedded in a qualitative case study. Stake's collective instrumental framing helps explain how multiple participant groups can illuminate a shared issue, while Sharan Merriam's constructivist case study model (2009, 2016) provides the methodological center for applied educational research.

Merriam's approach is suited for applied educational settings because it is particularistic (focusing on the classroom writing experience), descriptive (producing thick descriptions of pedagogical struggle), and heuristic (illuminating how teachers and students understand learning struggle).

Data Sources Overview

  • Proposed QUAL Practitioner: Merriam-style semi-structured interviews, a card sort protocol, and institutional document analysis.
  • Proposed QUAL Retrospective: University students recounting their high school transition (2022-2023).
  • quan (Educator Survey): General survey of K-12 teachers' perceived rigor vs. AI convenience.
  • quan (Secondary Datasets): Analyzing NCES School Pulse Panel data on national K-12 AI integration.
  • Supplementary AI comparison: Negative control comparing AI-generated teacher responses to human transcripts to outline human-specific pedagogical reasoning.

Methodology Flowchart (Merriam-Aligned)

Phase 1: Bounded Case Ingestion
• Establish boundaries of the case (K-12 writing instruction & teacher policy).
• Collect QUAL interviews & card sorts alongside quan surveys & NCES datasets.

Phase 2: Inductive Interpretative Analysis
• Code QUAL transcripts for meaning-making, focusing on noetic, rhetorical, and existential dimensions.
• Run appropriate quan descriptive and conditional comparative analyses.

Phase 3: Mixed Methods Integration
